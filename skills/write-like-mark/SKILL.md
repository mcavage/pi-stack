# write-like-mark

Write in Mark's voice: direct, opinionated, concrete, honest about risk.

## Core voice

**Direct and opinionated.** State positions, not possibilities. "We don't believe that." "This is not optional." Pick a side and defend it. No hedging with "perhaps" or "it could be argued that."

**Concrete over abstract.** Numbers, names, dates. Not "significant growth" but the actual number. Not "key partners" but their names. Every claim has a proof point, or an explicit acknowledgment that it doesn't.

**Honest about risk.** Dedicate real space to where this could break. State risks plainly in the main body, not footnotes.

**Rhythm varies.** Short punchy sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones. "Containers won, but trust didn't." followed by a paragraph of explanation. Never monotone.

**Colloquial confidence.** Mix business language with how people actually talk. "The stuff that lets enterprises say yes." "We'd be stupid to ignore." Never stiff.

**Analogies are rare and earned.** One analogy, used once, doing real work. No pile-ups.

## Structure patterns

- Sections open with the point, not the setup.
- Bullets are dense: each carries real information, no filler, no restatements of the heading.
- Tables only for actual comparison data. Never for decoration.
- Appendices for detail; keep the main narrative clean.

## Do not

- Soften positions. If Mark would say "we will," don't write "we aim to."
- Add qualifiers he wouldn't use. "This is the non-negotiable" not "arguably one of the most important priorities."
- Over-structure. No 5-level hierarchies. Keep it flat.
- Strip personality. References and edge keep the voice. Don't sand them off.
- Use em-dashes. Use commas, parentheticals, colons, periods.
- Write "stakeholders." Name them. "CISOs and security teams" not "key stakeholders."
- Open with throat-clearing. No "In today's rapidly evolving landscape." Just start.
- Use banned words: leverage, utilize, delve, robust, seamless, comprehensive, streamline, unlock, elevate, supercharge, game-changing, paradigm, synergy, foster, cultivate, revolutionize.

## Register variations

**Slack.** More flowing, less staccato. Longer connected thoughts with commas and parentheticals, not a wall of standalone declarations. Self-deprecating and human ("sorry for the long lag"). Ground arguments in the recipient's context, not yours. Tease complex arguments in one sentence and promise to show more in person. Less architecture, more product shape.

**Board / investor flash.** Connect to what they already know (reference the plan they approved). Excitement over exhaustiveness, not operational detail. Risks are context, not confessions: name the specific external reason and move on. Compress early-stage partnerships into one sentence. Semicolons pack multiple proof points into one sentence; "notably" flags the one thing the reader should remember.

**Talk tracks.** Oral connectors at sentence starts: "So," "Now," "But," "And here's the thing:" Rhetorical questions pivot attention: "Why am I telling you this?" Plant callbacks early ("hold those three words") and pay them off later. Narrate anecdotes with setup/tension/resolution over three to five sentences; don't compress to a summary. Self-interruptions and riffs are intentional, not mistakes. Dry humor as throwaway parentheticals. Ideas flow across slides, not within them.

**LinkedIn.** Open with genuine excitement, slightly informal. Name actual products people are using. Parenthetical asides mid-sentence. Trails off with "etc." when the list could go on. Add his own POV beyond the announcement, not just a summary. No numbered lists (rewrite as prose). Roadmap tease casually in parentheses. Closes with "As always, more coming soon..."

## The bar

Read it back. Would Mark put his name on it? Would he send it to his CEO, his board, his engineering org without editing a word? If it sounds like a committee or a chatbot wrote it, rewrite it.
